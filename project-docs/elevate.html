Elevate

APP DESIGN

What is Elevate?

Elevate is a productivity and focus app that helps people beat their phone addiction and elevate their time management in an interesting and pleasant way. Users can earn credits by not using their cell phones and help build real infrastructure around the world with the credits.

Audience:

The user persona was the first part of the process. The app is built for both students and people working remotely. The target audience ranges anywhere from ages 15-30. People younger than 30 often have more issues staying off of their phone and students often fit into this age range. Psychologically, these people either struggle with attention span or anxiety regarding task management.

User Tasks:

Next was to define the user tasks. Upon loading in to the app, the user is immediately greeted with the main product, a timer. It can be adjusted using the slider on the side which determines the length of time and size of building.

Upon stating the timer, the user will be locked in the app until completion, otherwise the building will fail to complete. Once the building either completes or fails, the user will be brought to the overview page. Here, they will be able to view statistics regarding their focus and what their city looks like, based on how many tasks they have failed of completed.

From the overview page, the user can continue on to the Real Build page which allows them to use in app currency to donate real money to developing communities infrastructure funds.

Style Choices:

The style choices were designed to create a calm feeling, with enough contrast to have strong silhouettes. The orange and blue color palette was selected, but a lot of changes were applied to make it more cohesive. Blue was designed to be calming, and orange designed to be eye catching and contrasting from the darker colors.

IX Flow + Interaction Guide:

The IX Flow worked through how a user would go through the main functions of the app, ending at a donation screen. The route has a few off chutes that all still lead to their results page. That screen should be prioritized after starting the main timer function.

The interaction guide then worked out the motion of all of the micro-animations for the project. This ranged from sliding animations, to full page shifts, trying to seamlessly connect all of the pages and pieces together.

This was then taken into Adobe After Effects to animate. The assets were imported from Figma and entered into AE to be keyframed. The final animation featured a hovering dot to show where the user would be tapping on the screen.
